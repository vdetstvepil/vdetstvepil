# My activity as a Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador
🧩 **Co-organizer of the project "Svoya IT-igra" for schoolchildren** <br />
The event “Svoya IT-igra” took place in Moscow (Russia) on November, 6 (2019). The event was devoted to the question how to increase curiosity of IT learners at school and how to encourage them to obtain new knowledge and acquire new skills in different IT-spheres. As for me, I took part in event as an organizer. I was responsible for the timetable, game, additional software and was also in charge of interacting with the participants. My colleague, Alexander Tsymbalov, also took part in organizing this event and was responsible for the content of the game and interaction with the audience during the event. The game was held in the Lyceum of Information Technologies. There were 25 participants. The format of the game was similar to one television game on Russian television channel NTV “Svoya Igra” (which is translated as “My Personal Game”). After the game the questionnaire was sent to the participants to get feedback. 100% of people were satisfied with the game and approved of the organizers’ work.

🧩 **Head of the project "Student Ambassadors Academy" for schoolchildren (9-12 grades in K-12)** <br />
Last summer (2020), I was looking for an idea for a new event that I could host for my previous school or university. I analyzed the situation and found out that there are no good C# programming courses in my city. That is, they are there, but they are not focused on teaching schoolchildren or students. For example, there is a very good program in Moscow - the Samsung IT School, where students are taught to develop mobile applications. Or courses from Yandex, where students are taught to develop in Python, but there are no popular courses on development in C#. This was my chance to prove myself and organize something big and cool. I put together a team of Microsoft learn student ambassadors and some of my good student friends, and together we organized an online course on C# development.

We promoted the event through some Moscow schools. We received about a hundred applications for participation in the course, conducted 60 interviews and selected about 40 applications. The participants of the course were mainly students of grades 9-12 in the K-12 system (In Russian education system: students in grades 8-11).

The course consisted of 6 modules of several classes. In total, there are 100 hours (40 classes) in the course. Students learned the programming language from scratch, gained experience in developing WPF applications, learned Git and GitHub, learned how to make bots with Azure Bot Services and Bot Framework, worked with databases in SQL, learned Unity, and gained basic knowledge in using machine learning with Azure to solve common tasks, gained experience working on team pr

On May 21 we held the final event, where we summed up the results of the course. 15 students graduated from the academy, received certificates and souvenirs from our community. This experiment was successful and we are going to launch new course from the Microsoft Learn Student Ambassadors community in September 2021!
